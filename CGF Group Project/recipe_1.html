Fruit Sponge Layer Cake

A chic revamp of an old staple, this recipe puts a spin on the classic Victoria Sandwich, topping with an assortment of sumptuous fruit

Prep Time:

30 minutes

Bake Time:

20 minutes

Ingredients:

  • 500g self-raising flour
  • 500g unsalted butter
  • 500g golden caster sugar
  • 5tsp baking powder
  • 9 large eggs
  • 3 lemons (grated zest)
  • 2 lemons (juice)
  • 600ml double cream
  • 3 figs
  • 50g blackberries
  • 50g blueberries

Method:

  1. Preheat the oven to 180°C/160°c fan/350°F/Gas Mark 4. Grease three 25cm round sandwich tins and line with greaseproof paper

  2. In a large bowl, add the softened butter and sugar cream together. Once completed, add eggs and both the lemon zest and juice. Next, sift the flour and baking powder into the bowl. Continue to mix together until smooth, fluffy and combined. If using an electric mixer, mix on a medium speed and do not over mix.
    3. l
  3. Divide the mixture between the three tins and place in the oven to bake for around 20 minutes or until the sponges are risen and golden. Once ready, remove the tins from the oven and turn out the sponges onto a wire rack to cool.

  4. While the sponges are cooling, whip the double cream thick enough to hold its shape. Spoon the whipped cream into a piping back with a round nozzle.

  5. Pipe a layer of double cream onto your base layer sponge and repeat this two more times until your cake is assembled. Ensure that your layers are stable and even.

  6. Chop and assemble your figs into different shapes and top onto the cake - to achieve our look, we cut one into slices, one quartered and one whole. Further embellish with the mixed berries. For an extra special look, add seasonal foliage.
